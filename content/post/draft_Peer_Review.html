The (many) problems with peer

  • The arXiv is good! It’s awesome to get access to important work in a relatively timely fashion, especially when the review process takes anywhere between months to years and exploits unpaid labor.

  • arXiv failure modes:

    • basically no quality control
    • too many papers
    • no comment feature, can only respond through another pre-print

-SciRate tries to tackle 1 & 3, but the overwhelming majority of papers receive no comment at all

  • In the year 2020 papers don’t need to be pdfs, or not just pdfs
  • Envision each paper as a git repo with the pdf as just one part (that can be posted to arXiv)
  • Data analysis could be stand-alone Jupyter notebooks, data can be downloaded and manipulated by others
  • The most insidious issues are subtle mistakes in important papers, the solutions to which are not publishable, but still cost other researchers a lot of time and money to diagnose and fix.
  • Contributions in the form of fixes/comments/reviews, etc are considered work.
  • Good contributions in any form weighted at least as strongly as ‘publication’ in performance reviews
  • Non-publishable analysis tricks/techniques/etc disseminate more quickly.
